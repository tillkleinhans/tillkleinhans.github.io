Landing page redesign

The CHECK24 Versicherungscenter (VC) simplifies managing and optimizing your insurances by digitizing and checking your contracts, and by providing personal service from insurance experts, all through a web and native app.

I received the task to redesign the landing page just two weeks after I started – therefore the challenge was to create a pitch for a product I’m completely new to, and managing the implementation of the redesign.

Status Quo

Originally, the landing page should be redesigned because the UI looks outdated and does not match the product’s UI. But it could also do a better job pitching the product as it takes too long to convey the product’s value, making it less convincing and motivating for users to follow our call to action.

User research – tell the right story

Understanding the users and product is crucial for a good pitch. That’s why I started by researching our users’ goals and pain points, and learning how our product’s value proposition responds to them. The following techniques helped me gather key insights:

1. Testing the product

I examined the product and analyzed which features respond to my own goals and pain points connected with insurances.

2. User interviews

Sadly interviewing real users is not common at CHECK24, so I conducted internal interviews with newcomers to get results as unbiased as possible.

3. Expert interviews

Luckily I had the chance to interview a managing director of the VC, and I also gathered the frequently asked questions from our customer agents.

4. Product analytics

I analyzed the performance and user behavior of the current landing page, so the CTR for each button, traffic sources, devices used and their screen resolution.

The user goals are...

  • Manage insurances digitally & accessible from everywhere
  • Get the optimal insurance protection
  • Save money

The pain points are...

  • Insurances are complex and difficult to understand
  • Uncertainty that product is free of charge, or if there are hidden costs
  • Concerns that the recommendations are biased
