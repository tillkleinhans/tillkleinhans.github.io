Landing page redesign

The CHECK24 insurance center simplifies managing and optimizing your insurances by digitizing and checking your contracts, and by providing personal service from insurance experts, all through a web and native app.
I received the task to redesign the landing page just two weeks after I started – therefore the challenge was to create a pitch for a product I’m completely new to, and managing the implementation of the redesign.

Role

UX/UI designer,
product manager

Team

Only myself

Duration

Feb – May 2021

Status Quo

Originally, the landing page should be redesigned because the UI looks outdated and does not match the product’s UI. Besides that, it could also do a better job pitching the product as it takes too long to convey the product’s value, making it less convincing and motivating for users to follow our call to action.

User research – tell the right story

Understanding the users and product is crucial for a good pitch. That’s why I started by researching our users’ goals and pain points, and learning how our product’s value proposition responds to them. The following techniques helped me gather key insights:

1. Testing the product

I examined the product and analyzed which features respond to my own goals and pain points connected with insurances.

2. User interviews

Sadly interviewing real users is not common at CHECK24, so I conducted interviews with colleagues and selected newcomers to get results as unbiased as possible.

3. Expert interviews

Luckily I had the chance to interview a managing director of the insurance center, and I also gathered the frequently asked questions from our customer agents.

4. Product analytics

I analyzed the performance and user behavior of the current landing page, specifically the traffic sources, click through rate, devices and screen resolutions used.

User goals and pain points

Our users want to manage insurances digitally & accessible from everywhere, get the optimal insurance protection, and save money along the way. They worry about the complexity and difficulty of insurances, biased recommendations from insurance brokers, and hidden costs.

Design iterations

I worked in iterations, from low-fidelity sketches to high-fidelity UI prototypes in Adobe XD. Between iterations I collected user feedback from colleagues, clarifying the technical feasibility of details with software developers, and discussed further direciton with business stakeholders. It was a special challenge to design a UI that is appealing, adjusts to the styleguide restrictions and aligns with a work-in-progress product UI.

Final design

Headline, subheadline and values address the user goals and pain points and encourage visitors to follow the centrally placed call to action.
