Winning with ideation

In 2019, I teamed up with two classmates to compete for 5000€ in a data science hackathon hosted by E.ON. We did not have any data science experience so far – unlike our competitors. Nevertheless, we managed to come in second place, beat 14 of 15 teams and win 1500€. Here is how we did it.

Challenge

For this hackathon, E.ON built a low-cost sensor board and collected an extensive dataset in a family home over the course of a month. We then had 48 hours to develop and pitch the best idea for E.ON's customer solution branch based on the data collected by these sensors:

Process

While the other teams started implementing an idea right away, we focused on ideation and invested our time into finding the right idea for E.ON and its customers.

💡 Discover quick ideas

We started by examining the capabilities of the various sensors and brainstorming how customers might benefit from them. Then we collected as many quick ideas as possible using sticky notes and ideation methods to keep the momentum going.

🔍 Explore use cases

Next, we clustered related ideas and created use cases from them. Then we discussed the desirability, viability and feasibility of the use cases together with business and technical stakeholders from E.ON, before selecting the best use cases for further development.

🚀 Check business models

We used the Business Model Canvas to assess the selected use cases and create comprehensive product concepts from them. Before deciding which product to pitch, we got together with a business stakeholder one last time to validate that we will present the best solution for E.ON and its customers.

Our product is a lifesaver

New parents give their all to keep their baby safe and healthy, but there is one thing they are helpless against and horrified of: Sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS). It's the sudden and unexplained death of a baby younger than 1 year old. Heat accumulation is one of few known factors increasing the risk for SIDS, a threat that is not checked by baby monitors on the market. With Halo, we are going to change this.

Meet Halo, the IoT-enhanced baby monitor

Halo helps parents take the best care of their baby by showing them unprecedented data about their baby and its environment, and alerting them via their smartphones in case of an emergency.

Minimize risk of SIDS

Automatically detect heat accumulations with a thermal imaging camera & receive alerts on your smartphone

Ensure healthy environmental conditions

Monitor the room's temperature, humidity, CO2 concentration & (potentially hazardous) organic compounds in the air

Monitor sleep & learn preferences

Supervise the baby's sleep cycle, expressed through movement and sounds & learn when it sleeps best

Even detect a full diaper!

A spike in organic compounds shows you when you need to brace yourself & go change the diaper

All these sensor data will be transmitted by Bluetooth Low Energy, the low-radiation standard used in the best baby monitors on the market, so parents don't have to worry about potential harms of the additional sensors.

Good reasons why E.ON believed in the idea

Halo is not only a great product, it’s also one with a promising future: Statista forecasts a growing IoT market with smart appliances as the largest segment and most customers between the ages of 25 and 34 – a common time in life to start a family, have a baby, and use a baby monitor like Halo.
