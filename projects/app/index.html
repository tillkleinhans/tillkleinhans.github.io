Extra mile for a better mobile app UX

Situation

As a UX designer for an automotive mobile app, I was part of 2 client feature teams embedded in our client's organization. One of the teams handled the feature that enables users to update their car's firmware themself – a process that spares our client the costly process of updating cars for their users in a workshop, but one that was facing usability challenges. Now, cars of the newest generation were sharing more information about the update process, which should be reflected in the mobile app.

Task

Extend feature with new functionality. The feature should be ready for the pre-Christmas testing with upper management in 12 weeks. PO is worried about time pressure & pushes for a UX concept that he created.

(Action)

Complete design brief

  • Understand UX concept proposed by PO → Extract what are hard requirements, what is negotiable proposal by PO
  • What new vehicle info?
  • Tech limitations

Challenge: Collaboration with PO

  • PO does not want to waste time
  • No trust yet, therefore pushes for own concept
  • Solution: Attune to PO's technical perspective, immer wieder auf Biz & Customer goals (= why we should do this) beziehen, Arbeitsstände teilen um PO's Vertrauen zu bekommen = er sieht dass etwas vorangeht

Redo concept

  • Highlight usability challenges in PO concept
  • What are tech limitations? → Ask PO & dev
    • Limited info from vehicle side: Started (trigger sent), 10% (confirmation: Vehicle started), 90% (return to vehicle), 100%
    • 16 conditions to start update: Handbremse, Batterie, Türen zu, nicht am Hang geparkt, etc.
  • Collaborate with UI designer to create coherent user flow visuals + take opportunity & fix usability problems in existing part (connection from phone to car) → Low-hanging fruit

Tech side: What is the new functionality, what are limitations?
