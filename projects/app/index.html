Extra mile for a better mobile app UX

Situation

As a UX designer, I was working in 2 feature teams in a project for a German car company and its mobile app. One team owned the feature to update the car's firmware via phone, and the latest generation cars enabled the starting and following installations by phone – a process that should be designed and implemented in the companion app.

Firmware updates: Counter-intuitive?

There are 3 ways for customers to update their car's firmware, with different cost implications for the manufacturer.

I – Download to phone & transfer to car

Albeit not being straightforward, this is the standard method for the manufacturer, as it uses the phone's Wi-Fi connection and does not come with extra costs.

II – Direct download to car

I expected this to be the standard method, and product analytics showed many users did so as well. It is more convenient for users, but it comes with an average of 5€ cellular costs per update for the manufacturer – amounting to more than 1 Million € due to the fleet's scale.

III – Installation in car workshop

As a fallback, firmware updates can be performed during workshop visits – causing 100€ extra cost per installation for the manufacturer.
