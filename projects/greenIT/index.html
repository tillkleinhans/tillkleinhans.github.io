Market-testing a cloud venture idea

From wind parks to data centers

In this 12-week project, a German energy corporation asked us to validate an ambitious venture idea:

Powering energy-intensive cloud computing services directly with energy from their own wind and solar parks, and selling them to other businesses.

The venture idea's lead was pushing for an investment, while the other stakeholders saw too much uncertainty. Eva (lead innovation consultant) and I were brought in to enable a decision.

Big market, strong competition

70-90% of the 100 Bn. € European cloud market is dominated by the seemingly invincible hyperscalers AWS, Azure and Google Cloud. But the outlook that only 1% of the non-hyperscaler market are worth 100 Mio. €, was a sufficient perspective to pursue this opportunity.

1% of the non-hyperscaler market is worth 100 Mio. €

Hypotheses & alignment on a goal

In order to be successful, the venture idea needed to fulfill the following 3 high-level hypotheses:

1. Feasible: We are capable to provide this service

Not in the project's scope, as the venture idea's lead explored the idea's feasibility in depth before this project.

2. Viable: We can offer this service for an attractive price

To validate this element, Eva collaborated with an external business analyst to calculate a business case with different scenarios.

3. Desirable: We can convince customers to buy the cloud service

To validate this element, we conducted a fake door test: We created a landing page prototype to test how attractive the service would be to real prospects.

The goal: 3 letters of intent

In the project kickoff, we agreed with our stakeholders on the goal of 3 customer letters of intent (LOI): If we managed to get 3 customers declaring their purchase interest in a LOI, we would perceive the desirability to be validated.

Research: Who is buying, and why?

To use the right keywords in our test, we researched which value propositions our competitors used to market their cloud services. While competitors used 1 or a combination of 2 of the values sustainability, high performance, and EU-sovereignty, the combination of the 3 values was an unoccupied positioning.

A combination of 3 value propositions gives a niche in the market

Customer segments & buyer profiles

To target the right people with our advertisement budget, we developed a rough set of customer segments and buyer profiles, using a combination of desk research and the project lead's experience from interaction with prospects.

  • Customer segments: Public sector, highly-confidential industries (e.g. automotive, pharma), other cloud-service providers
  • Profiles: CTOs, research/engineering department leads

Smart test setup for extra insights

Prospects should discover the service through ads on LinkedIn or tech blogs. A click leads to a landing page with optional topic subpages, convincing to fill out a contact form.

Different ads and subpages let us measure what prospects are interested in

Details for extra insights:

  • 2 ad versions, to compare click-through rates of the "sustainability" and "EU sovereignity" value propositions
  • Subpages for the 2 value propositions, for the same reason
  • Subpages for 3 hardware configurations, to inform investment decision

We would then use Calendly to schedule a video call, through which the client project lead would try to get one of 3 LOIs.

Clear test results & extra effort

With 4 weeks duration and 3000€ budget for LinkedIn and Google ads, we got these results:

None of the 3 required LOIs could be generated through the test

Instead of analyzing the details of such a clear result, we analyzed if we chose the wrong channel to sell an otherwise desirable idea.

Extra mile: Adding another channel

On a cloud tech trade fair in Frankfurt, we conducted quick interviews with industry experts to get qualitative insights about the desirability of our service.

Key insights:

  1. Customers would not choose our client over established cloud providers to run their (business-critical) cloud infrastructure
  2. Using renewable energy is already commoditized, and directly used renewable energy is not perceived better than when certificates are used → no differentiation
  3. EU sovereignty needed to be thought holistically: To stand out, a company had to refrain from any servers overseas – including those used to run the internal CMS, video conferencing and email.

Clear result, plus a way forward

The venture idea failed to induce 3 LOIs, disproving its desirability. Albeit not being the favored outcome, this 12-week project managed to enable an decision on more than 10 Mio. € of investment for a venture idea that stagnated internally for multiple years.

Turning away from cloud infrastructure, colocation showed potential:

Offering data centers without the cost-intensive hardware and hyperscaler competition, while keeping the venture's energy component.

This left the venture idea's lead, convinced by the validity of the insights that he co-created with us, with a potential path ahead.
