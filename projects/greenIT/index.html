Market-testing a cloud venture idea

Situation

In this short 12-week project, an energy corporation asked us to validate a venture idea in the cloud computing industry: Senior stakeholders were facing too much uncertainty in the idea's desirability and viability to make an investment decision.

My colleague Eva (lead business consultant) and I worked together with the client's project lead, who was pushing the idea internally for multiple years already.

The idea: Sustainable cloud computing

We want to power energy-intensive cloud computing services directly with energy from our own wind and solar parks and sell it to other businesses.

Market context: Isn't the competition too strong?

The competition in the cloud market is indeed very strong, as 70% of it is dominated by the three hyperscalers AWS, Microsoft Azure and Google [1]. But the rational is: Even a little share of a TODO: market size in bn. € market ([2]<- link here) is attractive to run a successful business in.

Preparation

To start this project, we had to understand what exactly we are offering, what competition we had to differentiate ourself from, and who are we selling to.

IaaS: Not all clouds are the same

Do you know what Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) is? It is a cloud service model that other models build on top of in the cloud stack:

  1. Physical data centers, minus the servers
  2. Infrastructure-aaS: Physical servers, plus virtualization software
  3. Platform-aaS: Development software (e.g. Kubernetes, databases)
  4. Software-aaS: User software-as-a-Service (e.g. Slack, Microsoft Teams)

IaaS was what the project lead was aiming for, supplemented with a few PaaS add-ons that the technical partners could provide.

The project lead focused on sustainability and high-power computing as value propositions.

Sustainability

Everyone talks about sustainability, hyperscalers and non-hyperscalers. There is a difference between green energy through compensation with certificates and direct use of green energy, but we have to see how important this differentiation is for customers. Also, there are competitors that focus on sustainability holistically, with using second hand hardware and farming algaes with the excess server heat.

High-power computing

Average powered cloud infrastructure is sufficient for most current use cases. Other cases like artificial intelligence (AI) or high-performance computing (HPI) require more power, and there are a few competitors that focus on these cases.

Additionally: EU-sovereignity

My colleague Eva identified an additional focus in our competitors' value propositions: EU-sovereignity. It's relevant because of GDPR requirements.
